Practical Skills

How to Talk to AI — Prompt Engineering 101

The difference between a mediocre AI response and a brilliant one is all in how you ask.

✗ Vague Prompt
"Write me an email about the invoice"
→ Generic, unfocused output that needs heavy editing
✓ Engineered Prompt
"You are a professional accountant at an Australian firm. Write a polite follow-up email to a client whose invoice #4521 is 14 days overdue. Keep it firm but friendly. Use Australian English."
→ Specific, actionable, ready to send
1
Give it a role — "You are a senior tax advisor"
2
Be specific — Include context, constraints, and format
3
Show examples — Provide a sample of what "good" looks like
4
Iterate — Refine the output through follow-up prompts
Level Up

Prompting Like a Pro

Six techniques that separate good AI output from brilliant AI output.

5
Specify your format — "Give me a numbered list, under 200 words, with a one-line summary at the top."
6
Set constraints — "Australian English. No legal jargon. Under 200 words."
7
Think step by step — Add "reason through this before answering" for anything involving logic or numbers.
8
Give context first — Paste the email thread, document, or background before asking your question.
9
Ask for alternatives — "Give me 3 versions: one formal, one casual, one brief."
10
Stack role + audience — "Act as a senior tax advisor explaining this to a client with no finance background."
🎯 Pro move: Chain them all — role + context + constraints + format + audience = output you can send as-is.

Stay Safe

AI Safety & Best Practices

Non-negotiable rules for using AI in professional services.

1
Never share confidential data
Client names, financials, and PII must never go into free public AI tools
2
Always verify the numbers
AI can hallucinate figures confidently — check every calculation yourself
3
Know your firm's AI policy
Confirm which tools are approved and what data classification rules apply
4
Use enterprise tools for client work
Microsoft Copilot and Google Workspace AI are designed for business data security
5
Disclose AI use when it matters
Transparency builds trust — be ready to say when AI materially contributed to your work
